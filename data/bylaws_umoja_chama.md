# UMOJA CHAMA — BYLAWS & CONSTITUTION
**Registered under the Societies Act (Cap. 108), Republic of Kenya**
**Registration No.: CHAMA/NRB/2021/0847**
**Date of Adoption: 14 March 2021 | Last Amended: 02 January 2024**

---

## ARTICLE 1 — NAME AND SEAT

1.1 The organisation shall be known as **Umoja Chama** (hereinafter "the Chama").
1.2 The principal office shall be in Nairobi, Kenya. Meetings may be held at any agreed venue within Nairobi County.

---

## ARTICLE 2 — OBJECTIVES

2.1 To promote a culture of regular saving among members.
2.2 To extend affordable credit (merry-go-round loans) to members.
2.3 To invest pooled funds in agreed ventures for collective benefit.
2.4 To resolve member disputes fairly, transparently, and without recourse to courts where possible.

---

## ARTICLE 3 — MEMBERSHIP

3.1 **Eligibility.** Any adult Kenyan citizen of good moral character may apply. Existing members must vote on each application; a two-thirds majority is required for admission.

3.2 **Maximum membership.** The Chama shall not exceed **20 active members** at any time.

3.3 **Membership fee.** A one-time joining fee of **KES 2,000** is payable upon admission. This fee is non-refundable.

3.4 **Resignation.** A member wishing to resign must give **30 days' written notice**. Outstanding loans must be cleared before exit. The member's share of the welfare fund is forfeited; their savings and any declared dividends are refunded within 60 days.

3.5 **Expulsion.** A member may be expelled by a three-quarters vote of all members for: (a) three consecutive contribution defaults, (b) proven fraud or misappropriation, (c) bringing the Chama into disrepute. An expelled member forfeits the joining fee and welfare contributions; savings and dividends are refunded within 90 days after any outstanding obligations are offset.

---

## ARTICLE 4 — GOVERNANCE

4.1 **Officials.** The Chama shall be led by:
- **Chairperson** — presides over meetings, casts a deciding vote on ties.
- **Secretary** — maintains minutes, correspondence, and the member register.
- **Treasurer** — custodian of funds, maintains accounts, issues receipts.
- **Welfare Coordinator** — manages the welfare fund and disbursements.

4.2 **Election.** Officials are elected annually at the January Annual General Meeting (AGM) by secret ballot. No official may serve more than **two consecutive terms** in the same position.

4.3 **Quorum.** A quorum for any meeting is **two-thirds (⅔) of all active members**. No binding resolution may be passed without quorum.

4.4 **AGM.** Held every January. Agenda must include: financial statements, election of officials, and approval of the investment plan.

4.5 **Special General Meeting (SGM).** Called by the Chairperson or upon written request by at least **five members**. Notice of at least **seven days** must be given.

---

## ARTICLE 5 — CONTRIBUTIONS

5.1 **Monthly contribution.** Each member shall contribute **KES 5,000** on or before the **5th of every month** via M-Pesa to the Chama's official paybill: **Paybill No. 522533, Account: UMOJA[member ID]**.

5.2 **Late payment.** A contribution received between the 6th and 15th of the month incurs a **KES 200 late fee**. Payment after the 15th incurs a **KES 500 late fee**.

5.3 **Default.** Failure to contribute for a given month without prior written notice to the Secretary constitutes a **default**. Three consecutive defaults trigger a review under Article 3.5.

5.4 **Exemption.** A member may apply to the Chairperson for a contribution exemption due to documented medical emergency or retrenchment. Approval requires a majority vote and exemption does not count as a default but does not accrue savings for that month.

5.5 **Welfare contribution.** In addition to the monthly saving, each member shall contribute **KES 500/month** to the Welfare Fund.

---

## ARTICLE 6 — MERRY-GO-ROUND (ROTATING FUND)

6.1 The monthly pool (gross contributions minus any deductions) rotates among members in an order determined by draw at the January AGM each year.

6.2 The rotation order may be swapped between two consenting members with the **written approval of the Chairperson and Treasurer**. The swap must be recorded in the minutes.

6.3 A member who has received the merry-go-round payout remains obligated to continue contributing until all other members have received theirs.

6.4 If the member scheduled to receive the payout has an outstanding loan balance exceeding **50% of the payout amount**, the Treasurer shall offset the loan before releasing the remainder.

---

## ARTICLE 7 — LOANS

7.1 **Eligibility.** A member must have contributed consistently for at least **three months** before applying for a loan.

7.2 **Maximum loan.** A member may borrow up to **three times their current savings balance**.

7.3 **Interest rate.** Loans attract **5% flat interest per month** on the outstanding principal.

7.4 **Repayment period.** Maximum repayment period is **six months**. Extensions require Treasurer and Chairperson approval.

7.5 **Guarantors.** Every loan above KES 10,000 requires **two guarantors** who are active members in good standing. Guarantors are jointly and severally liable if the borrower defaults.

7.6 **Default on loan.** If a member misses two consecutive loan repayments, the Treasurer shall notify the guarantors in writing. If the loan remains unpaid after 30 days of that notice, the outstanding balance shall be deducted from the borrower's savings and guarantors' savings (proportionally) with the approval of the Chairperson.

---

## ARTICLE 8 — WELFARE FUND

8.1 The Welfare Fund is maintained separately from the main savings pool.

8.2 **Disbursements:**
- Death of member or immediate family (spouse/child/parent): **KES 10,000**
- Hospitalisation exceeding 3 days: **KES 5,000** (once per calendar year)
- Member's wedding: **KES 3,000** (once per membership)

8.3 Claims must be submitted with supporting documents (death certificate, hospital discharge summary, marriage certificate) within **60 days** of the event.

8.4 The Welfare Coordinator may reject unsubstantiated claims. A rejected claimant may appeal to the full membership at the next meeting.

---

## ARTICLE 9 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 **Informal resolution.** Any dispute between members or between a member and the Chama shall first be referred to the **Chairperson** for informal mediation within 14 days of the dispute arising.

9.2 **Mediation panel.** If informal resolution fails, a **Mediation Panel** comprising the Secretary, Welfare Coordinator, and one member elected by the disputing parties shall hear the matter. The panel shall issue a written decision within **21 days**.

9.3 **AI-assisted arbitration.** The Chama adopts the use of an AI arbitration tool ("the Arbitrator") to assist the Mediation Panel. The Arbitrator may be invoked by the Treasurer or Chairperson. It shall be given access to: these bylaws, M-Pesa transaction records, and the contribution ledger. Its output is advisory; the Mediation Panel retains final authority.

9.4 **Appeal.** A party dissatisfied with the Mediation Panel's decision may appeal to a Special General Meeting convened under Article 4.5. The SGM decision is final and binding within the Chama.

9.5 **External arbitration.** Only after internal processes are exhausted may a member seek external arbitration under the Arbitration Act (Cap. 49) or approach the Business Registration Service.

---

## ARTICLE 10 — FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT

10.1 The Chama shall maintain a bank account at a licensed Kenyan bank. Two signatories (Chairperson + Treasurer) are required for any withdrawal above **KES 20,000**.

10.2 The Treasurer shall present a financial statement at every monthly meeting.

10.3 An independent audit shall be conducted annually before the AGM. The auditor shall be appointed by majority vote and shall not be a current member.

10.4 The financial year runs from **1 January to 31 December**.

---

## ARTICLE 11 — AMENDMENTS

11.1 These bylaws may be amended only at an AGM or SGM with at least **14 days' written notice** of the proposed amendment.

11.2 Amendments require a **three-quarters majority** of all active members.

---

## ARTICLE 12 — DISSOLUTION

12.1 The Chama may be dissolved by a unanimous vote of all active members at an SGM called for that purpose.

12.2 Upon dissolution, all outstanding loans shall be called in, all liabilities settled, and the remaining assets distributed among members in proportion to their total contributions over the lifetime of the Chama.
