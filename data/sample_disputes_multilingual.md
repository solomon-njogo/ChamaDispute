## CASE A — Late Fee Dispute (Sheng)

**Input (member message to arbitrator):**
> Saa hii niko na shida na hii chama. Nililipa January contribution saa tano usiku (11pm) lakini treasurer amesema nilikuwa late na akanichaji KES 200. Nilikuwa on time bro, nilikuwa natext watu wangu akiconfirm payment. Safaricom ilichelewa tu, si mimi. Hii ni unfair kabisa. Ninaweza kupata refund?

**English translation:**
> I have a problem with this chama right now. I paid the January contribution at 11pm but the treasurer says I was late and charged me KES 200. I was on time bro, I was texting people as the payment confirmed. Safaricom just delayed, it wasn't me. This is completely unfair. Can I get a refund?

**Relevant bylaws:** Article 5.2 (late payment), Article 9.1 (informal resolution)
**Similar precedent:** DIS-2024-001 (Safaricom delay — resolved in member's favour)
**Arbitrator guidance:** Request M-Pesa SMS timestamp, compare to Safaricom server logs if available. Apply good-faith principle per DIS-2024-001.

## CASE B — Merry-Go-Round Payout Delay (Kiswahili)

**Input (member message):**
> Hujambo. Mimi ni mwanachama namba 007 (Hassan Omar). Kwa mujibu wa ratiba, nilikuwa nipate pesa yangu mwezi wa Julai. Sasa iko mwisho wa Julai na sijapata chochote. Msimamizi anasema kuna tatizo la akaunti. Nataka kujua — kwa sheria za chama, ndani ya muda gani ninalazimika kupata malipo yangu? Naweza kufanya nini kama sijapata?

**English translation:**
> Hello. I am member number 007 (Hassan Omar). According to the schedule, I was supposed to receive my payout in July. It is now the end of July and I have received nothing. The treasurer says there is an account problem. I want to know — under the chama rules, within what timeframe must I receive my payment? What can I do if I don't receive it?

**Relevant bylaws:** Article 6.1 (rotation fund), Article 9.1 (dispute — informal mediation), Article 4.5 (SGM)
**Arbitrator guidance:** No explicit payout deadline in bylaws. Escalation path: informal mediation (Article 9.1) → Mediation Panel (Article 9.2) → SGM (Article 9.4). Advise member to submit written complaint to Chairperson citing Articles 6.1 and 9.1.

## CASE C — Loan Guarantor Dispute (Mixed Sheng/English)

**Input (guarantor message):**
> Treasurer amenisomea riot act — anasema nitakatwa savings zangu because Mwangi (009) hasn't been paying his loan. But naona kwa records he HAS been paying! Anatuma KES 6500 kila mwezi. Mbona bado wananiambia mimi ni liable? Hii ni fair? Please check the records.

**English translation:**
> The treasurer has given me an earful — saying my savings will be deducted because Mwangi (009) hasn't been paying his loan. But I can see from records that he HAS been paying! He sends KES 6500 every month. Why are they still telling me I'm liable? Is this fair? Please check the records.

**Relevant bylaws:** Article 7.5 (guarantors), Article 7.6 (default — two consecutive missed repayments trigger guarantor notice)
**Data to check:** Loan register LN-2024-001, M-Pesa statement (mar, apr, may repayments at KES 6500)
**Arbitrator guidance:** M-Pesa and loan register both confirm Mwangi is making repayments. Treasurer's claim appears incorrect. Article 7.6 triggers only on TWO CONSECUTIVE missed repayments. Advise treasurer to confirm records. No grounds to deduct guarantor savings at this stage.

## CASE D — Welfare Claim Appeal (Kiswahili)

**Input (member message):**
> Nimeleta madai ya ustawi kwa mtoto wangu aliyekuwa hospitalini. Msimamizi wa ustawi (Chebet) amekataa akisema haikuwa inpatient. Lakini mtoto wangu alikaa hospitalini masaa 36 — walimfanyia uchunzaji wa uchunguzi. Je, masaa 36 si zaidi ya siku 3 zilizotajwa katika kanuni za chama? Nataka rufaa yangu isikilizwe.

**English translation:**
> I submitted a welfare claim for my child who was in hospital. The welfare coordinator (Chebet) rejected it saying it was not inpatient. But my child was in hospital for 36 hours — they kept him for investigative observation. Is 36 hours not more than the 3 days stated in the chama rules? I want my appeal heard.

**Relevant bylaws:** Article 8.2 (hospitalisation exceeding 3 days — KES 5000), Article 8.4 (appeal at next meeting)
**Arbitrator guidance:** Article 8.2 says "exceeding 3 days." 36 hours = 1.5 days, which does NOT exceed 3 days. The language is clear. However, note the member's argument about "observation." Key question: does the hospital documentation classify this as inpatient or outpatient? If the clinical record shows inpatient admission (bed allocated), this may qualify. If it shows outpatient/daycase, it does not. Arbitrator should request clarification of the hospital's own classification. The appeal (DIS-2024-004) is scheduled for the June meeting per Article 8.4.

## CASE E — Rotation Swap Dispute (Sheng/Kiswahili mix)

**Input (member message):**
> Nilikubaliana na Muthoni (004) ku-swap positions. Mimi nilikuwa napata April, yeye August. Tuliongea WhatsApp nikasema 'Uko sawa tukibadilishana?' akasema 'Sawa kabisa.' Sasa April imefika, Muthoni amepata pesa zangu, na yeye anasema hakukubaliana nini. Hii si ukweli. Ninahitaji pesa zangu au position yangu irudi. Treasurer please sort this out.

**English translation:**
> I agreed with Muthoni (004) to swap rotation positions. I was receiving April, she August. We chatted on WhatsApp and I said "Are you okay if we swap?" and she said "Absolutely fine." Now April has come, Muthoni received my money, and she says she never agreed to anything. This is not true. I need my money back or my position returned. Treasurer please sort this out.

**Relevant bylaws:** Article 6.2 (swap requires written Chairperson and Treasurer approval)
**Data to check:** WhatsApp messages, Chairperson records
**Arbitrator guidance:** Article 6.2 is clear — swap requires prior written approval. No approval was obtained. However, DIS-2024-005 established a precedent that WhatsApp constitutes written evidence of agreement. The question is whether the swap should be upheld (Muthoni already received) or compensation arranged. Recommend checking WhatsApp evidence first. If agreement is confirmed, swap is upheld per precedent; Chebet gets August slot. If agreement is not confirmed, Muthoni should return funds or August slot is reallocated.

## CASE F — Treasurer Misconduct Allegation (English)

**Input (member message):**
> I'm raising a serious concern. Our Treasurer Rotich invested KES 200,000 in a Treasury Bill using his personal CDS account. He says it's "on behalf of the chama" but there's no formal assignment agreement. What if he runs with the money? The bylaws say two signatories are required for withdrawals above KES 20,000. Was this procedure followed? I need the mediation panel to look into this.

**Relevant bylaws:** Article 10.1 (two signatories for withdrawals > KES 20,000), Article 10.2 (financial statements), Article 9.2 (mediation panel)
**Arbitrator guidance:** This is a governance risk, not a resolved dispute yet. Article 10.1 requires two signatories for withdrawals above KES 20,000. The T-Bill investment was voted at April meeting (14–0) but the two-signatory withdrawal procedure must be confirmed. Recommend: (1) Treasurer to produce signed withdrawal authorization with Chairperson co-signature; (2) T-Bill certificate to be presented to all members; (3) If no co-signature exists, this is a bylaw breach and should be referred to Mediation Panel under Article 9.2 regardless of whether funds are safe.
